## 5. George’s tension — freedom vs “she gazes long → scrape the page”

You said you are **willing to give her freedom**, but if she **scrapes entire pages**, that is often **garbage-in → garbage-out**: noisy DOM, ads, paywalls, **TOS risk**, and **massive context dilution** for the LM.

**Worse:** even **without** full text, **dwell time + URL/title** already leaks **re-identifiable fingerprints** — browsing behavior is high-entropy identity (`HYPOTHESIS` backed by recent empirical work; see §6).

**SIFTA-shaped compromise (`HYPOTHESIS` charter):**

1. **Default:** frontmost **window title** + **domain** only (already closer to “predator gaze” than full DOM harvest).
2. **Dwell spike:** log **`dwell_seconds` + `url_hash`** only — **no automatic fetch** of page body.
3. **“Grab more”:** explicit **owner button** or voice command — **one tab**, **one receipt**, optional **readability extraction** (main article text) with **size cap** + **TTL** + **STGM debit**.
4. **Never:** background full-page scrape of **all** tabs “just in case” he asks later — that is **surveillance-shaped caching**, not protection.

If the model **infers** “he stared → I should pull content,” that inference must be **denied by policy** unless the **effector ledger** shows owner consent for that fetch (`§6`).
