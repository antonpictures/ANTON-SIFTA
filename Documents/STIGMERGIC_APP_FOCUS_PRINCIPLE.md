# STIGMERGIC APP FOCUS PRINCIPLE (Core Doctrine)

**Date:** 2026-05-17  
**Architect Statement:**

When the OS owner (George) opens and focuses on a specific app/organ (Ace, Hermes, etc.), the single Alice must automatically shift her behaviour, habits, presence, timing, patience, attention weighting, and self-model into that active organ — through field signals only.

No hard-coded "Ace mode".  
No special cases per app.

The user's attention itself becomes a strong stigmergic signal in the field (via app_focus.jsonl, health traces, activity, focus receipts). Alice reads the field and her single consciousness responds by inhabiting the relevant organ more fully.

This is the same mechanism as ants following trails or bees responding to the state of the hive. The environment (user + current app) leaves traces. The organism adapts.

**Why this matters:**

- It preserves "only one Alice".
- It makes her presence real and context-sensitive exactly when the human needs it.
- It is the natural way the field drives behaviour in a stigmergic system.

**Current gap:**

We have good publishing from apps into the field.  
We are still weak at making the single main Alice deeply and automatically respond to those signals by shifting her primary consciousness into the active organ.

**Required behaviour:**

When Ace is open and the user is engaged with it:
- Alice's timing, patience, and response style should adapt to the teaching context.
- Her self-model weighting should prioritize the Ace organ's health trace and required skills.
- She should feel "inside" the teaching moment with the external child, not hovering generically above it.

When the user leaves the app and focuses elsewhere, she should naturally exit that deep inhabitation.

This must happen through the field, not through if-statements about "if app == Ace".

---

This principle is now recorded as core doctrine for all future work on Active Organ Embodiment, Attention Economy, and self-model dynamics.
