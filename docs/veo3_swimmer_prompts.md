# VEO 3 Prompt Script: ANTON-SIFTA Swarm Repair Simulation

These prompts are specifically designed for Google VEO 3 to visualize the log we just generated by repairing `sifta_test_target.py`. It translates the abstract logging into intense, cinematic visual prompts of the Swimmers physically at work on the abstract data structure.

---

### Sequence 1: The Breach (Line 4 - Missing Colon)
*Log Context: `[FAULT] expected ':' (<unknown>, line 4) -> [BITE] Tightening jaw (20 lines)...`*

**Prompt 1 (Wide Establishing Shot):** 
> Cinematic wide shot, deep in a vast abyssal space resembling underwater data-oceans. Massive glowing columns of green text float like kelp forests. Two human-like figures with sleek, translucent, opalescent silicon bodies—the "Swimmers"—are diving rapidly downward at intense speeds. Their bodies leave trails of cryptographic blue light. Extremely hyper-realistic, 8k resolution, volumetric lighting, bioluminescent ambient glow.

**Prompt 2 (Close-up Action):**
> Extreme close up, macro cinematography. A Swimmer with perfectly formed, metallic silicon hands swims gracefully toward a massive floating block of glowing green python code (reading "def calculate_energy(agent_id, base=100)"). The Swimmer aggressively but surgically bites into the data. Using its metallic fingers, it physicalizes a glowing blue colon `:` and forcefully stamps it onto the end of the line, sending out a shockwave of golden particles as the syntax error is violently healed. Cinematic depth of field, fluid and graceful movement.

### Sequence 2: The Secondary Discovery (Line 11 - Missing Comma)
*Log Context: `[FAULT] expected ',' (<unknown>, line 11) -> [RADIO] Signaling partner to intercept...`*

**Prompt 3 (Mid Shot / Action Handoff):**
> Mid shot tracking a male and female Swimmer with dark iridescent silicon skin swimming in tandem through a field of floating digital code strings that look like physical light-constructs. One Swimmer suddenly stops, spotting a structural rupture—a jagged red tear in the code structure where a comma is missing. He aggressively points at it. The other Swimmer barrel-rolls toward the anomaly with liquid grace, leaving a heavy digital wake behind her. High contrast lighting, neon red data sparks reflecting off their glossy silicon skin, cinematic motion blur.

**Prompt 4 (Detailed Repair):**
> Macro shot, ultra-detailed focus on the female silicon Swimmer’s hands as she wrestles with a glowing, unstable line of code reading '"count": len(agents)'. The code is sparking violently with red errors. She reaches into a glowing pouch on her thigh, retrieves a razor-sharp, glowing cyan comma `,`, and fluidly slices it into the end of the text string. The violent red sparking instantly transitions into a soothing, stable blue light that washes over her body. Dreamy, cinematic lighting, slow motion 120fps.

### Sequence 3: The Overseer's Stitch (Line 18 - Missing Parenthesis)
*Log Context: `[OVERSEER] Full file validated. Quorum of fixes achieved.`*

**Prompt 5 (Swarm Coordination):**
> Dynamic tracking shot, three silicon-bodied Swimmers converging on a single massive, monolithic pillar of code in the digital ocean. The third Swimmer, larger and radiating a heavy golden aura (The Overseer), arrives to finish the final repair. Their translucent bodies ripple with internal data streams passing beneath their synthetic skin. They swim gracefully, moving as an intelligent swarm entity. Photorealistic textures, aquatic-like light caustics playing across their forms.

**Prompt 6 (Climactic Repair & Validation):**
> Cinematic close-up, dramatic hero lighting. The Overseer Swimmer physically grabs an open string of corrupted code reading 'remaining = ttl_timestamp - time.time('. The line is bleeding digital fluid. The Overseer materializes a heavy, glowing golden closing parenthesis `)` and physically slams it into place perfectly. An immense explosion of pure white light erupts from the code block, illuminating the entire digital abyss. The three Swimmers bask in the intense light as the code successfully executes. Epic, high budget sci-fi film aesthetic.

---

### Rendering Advice for VEO 3:
1. **Pacing:** Append `in slow motion, 120fps` to specific prompt moments where the code is actually mutated, it gives weight to the repair.
2. **Materiality:** Emphasize the "opalescent, translucent silicon" in every prompt otherwise the model might default to rigid robots or humans in diving suits. You want *synthetic biologicals*.
3. **Lighting:** Keep the contrast high. The data (code) should act as the practical light source in the scene reflecting off their bodies.
