### 2. Key Scientific and Economic Papers

#### A. The Ontology of Social Objects
**Maurizio Ferraris, *Documentality: Why It Is Necessary to Leave Traces* (2012).**
*   **Core Thought:** Ferraris argues that social reality is built on "inscribed acts." Both money and identity are "social objects." However, money is an inscription of *value* (transferable), while identity is an inscription of *responsibility* (non-transferable). The "Cryptobody" is the ultimate documentary object—a digital trace that defines the self through its immutable record.

#### B. Money as Pure Abstraction
**Georg Simmel, *The Philosophy of Money* (1900/1907).**
*   **Core Thought:** Simmel posits that money facilitates the distancing between the subject and the object. As money becomes more abstract (from gold to digital bits), it becomes more "ethereal." It transcends the individual life, achieving a form of immortality. Money is the "pure form" of social interaction, stripped of specific identity.

#### C. The Informational Nature of Identity
**Luciano Floridi, *The Fourth Revolution: How the Infosphere is Reshaping Human Reality* (2014).**
*   **Core Thought:** Floridi introduces the "onlife" and the "informational organism" (inforg). The Cryptobody is an informational entity within the infosphere. The distinction here is that while Money is a **quantitative** informational signal (how much), Identity is a **qualitative** structural signal (who/what).

#### D. The Legal Construction of Capital
**Katharina Pistor, *The Code of Capital: How the Law Creates Wealth and Inequality* (2019).**
*   **Core Thought:** Pistor identifies how "mere" assets are transformed into "capital" through legal coding. This applies to the Cryptobody: an identity becomes a "body" only when it is coded with rights and persistence. Money is the result of this coding; the Cryptobody is the vessel that holds the code.

#### E. Transactional Psychology and The Social Life of Money
**Viviana Zelizer, *The Social Meaning of Money* (1994).**
*   **Core Thought:** Zelizer debunks the idea of money as a uniform, neutral tool. She argues that people "earmark" money based on identity and social relationships. This suggests the Cryptobody and Money are inextricably linked: the *source* of the money (identity) changes the *nature* of the money.
